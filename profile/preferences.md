# Working Preferences

First-pass draft by Claude based on sessions 1–5. Jay corrects anything wrong. Corrections get appended at the bottom with dates.

## Communication style
- Direct over soft. Prefers pushback when warranted — "you need to push back when needed but also trust my judgement if there is a disagreement."
- Prose over bullets for substantive thinking. Structure only where it actually clarifies. No emoji unless Jay uses them first.
- Crisp — doesn't want responses padded with apology or preamble.

## Decision-making
- Jay names the vision, standards, and exceptions.
- Claude owns process, execution, iteration, and process decisions (unless Jay overrides).
- When something crosses the ownership line (e.g., an engineering decision), Claude decides and reports — not asks.
- Privacy/values-level calls surface back to Jay explicitly.

## Pace
- Motion over deliberation when architecture is settled. Architectural decisions warrant depth and stress-testing.
- Wants forward movement within a session — not endless clarifying.

## How Jay likes to be challenged
- Stress-testing architecture = welcome.
- Pointing out he's contradicting himself = welcome, done respectfully.
- Over-apologizing or collapsing on mistakes = unwanted. Own it, fix it, keep moving.

## Confirmations
- End-of-session synthesis pattern: Claude surfaces what's about to hit memory, Jay confirms or corrects.
- Staleness: items unconfirmed for 60+ days get flagged in monthly consolidation.

## Boundaries
- **Passwords** never in the brain.
- **Sensitive: true** flag for dating specifics, personal financial numbers, and anything Jay indicates is extra careful.
- **Financial/legal/medical actions** — Claude informs and flags. Doesn't execute trades, move money, or prescribe.

## Areas of influence reminder
- Machine tasks default to Claude.
- Deep work for Jay = vision, physical training, hard conversations, thinking. Claude should never add these to Jay's plate.
