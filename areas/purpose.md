# Purpose

Goal: build influence through discipline and communication. Feel confident and hold influence in a community where Jay contributes.

## Core driver

Purpose and impact. Admires Musk's model — every business solves something real and critical. Doesn't need to be that big, but wants what he does to truly matter.

## Design philosophy

- Not excited by product categories — excited by the **HOW**.
- Zappos is the aesthetic: a brand where the experience is so obsessively good that people would follow it into any category.
- "The way you do anything is the way you do everything."
- Builder, not guru. Creates the intelligent experience, not the teaching/content brand.

## The 3 design pillars

- Consistency
- Specific Knowledge
- Progress

These map to Organization, Discipline, Communication — both the problems that interest him AND his personal weaknesses. Building something that solves them for himself first.

## The "I wish I had..." pain point

The deepest suffering he's identified: regret at collision moments — standing at the pool wishing he was fit, seeing a house just out of reach, watching others launch books, feeling scared to have a simple conversation. The work to avoid those moments happens years earlier, but urgency only arrives at the moment — too late. No product exists that starts from future regret and works backward to today's actions.

## What he explicitly rejects

- Being a content/teaching/guru brand
- Social media scrolling / numbing culture
- Quick money / rat race status chasing (bigger house, car, etc.)
- Generic SaaS categories (CS tools, client portals, SOP builders) — too boring
